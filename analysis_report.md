# Movie Memory Experiment Findings

## Overview
This report summarizes the results of the BRSM Movie Memory Experiment, which investigated whether interrupting videos abruptly before a natural event boundary impairs subsequent recognition memory. Two groups were analyzed:
*   **Natural Cut (NB)**: Participants watched uninterrupted videos.
*   **Abrupt Cut (AB)**: Participants watched videos interrupted 1-5 seconds before an event boundary.

Data from 171 participant logs was collected. After excluding formatting errors and applying a vigilance threshold, 171 valid participants were included in the final analysis (NB: 91 participants, AB: 80 participants). Because the task was 2-Alternative Forced Choice (2AFC), recognition memory (REC) was scored simply using hit rates (accuracy).

### Statistical Results

We performed independent t-tests comparing the average performance between the two groups. 

**1. Overall Recognition Accuracy**
The Natural Cut group performed significantly better overall at recognizing frames they had previously seen compared to the Abrupt Cut group.
*   **NB:** 87.06% Accuracy
*   **AB:** 83.87% Accuracy
*   **Result:** \`$t = 2.67$, $p = 0.008$\` (**Significant**)

**2. Before Boundary (BB) Frame Accuracy**
When participants were tested specifically on frames that occurred right before an event shift, the Natural Cut group again performed significantly better. This supports the hypothesis that abrupt editing disrupts the encoding of the concluding moments of an event.
*   **NB:** 85.99% Accuracy
*   **AB:** 82.25% Accuracy
*   **Result:** \`$t = 2.43$, $p = 0.016$\` (**Significant**)

**3. Event Middle (EM) Frame Accuracy**
Even for frames taken from the middle of continuous events (far from the interrupted boundaries), the Natural Cut group retained a small but statistically significant advantage over the Abrupt Cut group.
*   **NB:** 88.13% Accuracy
*   **AB:** 85.50% Accuracy
*   **Result:** \`$t = 1.99$, $p = 0.048$\` (**Significant**)

**4. Response Times & Confidence**
There was no statistically significant difference between the two groups regarding how quickly they answered (Response Time) or how confident they felt about their answers.
*   **RT:** NB (5.80s) vs AB (5.57s) - \`$p = 0.33$\` (*Not Significant*)
*   **Confidence:** NB (4.20) vs AB (4.08) - \`$p = 0.07$\` (*Not Significant*)

## Conclusion
The data conclusively supports the hypothesis that **Abrupt cuts impair memory encoding**. 
Participants who watched Natural Cuts had significantly higher recognition accuracy for both boundary-specific frames and general event frames, confirming that interrupting the established "event schema" before its natural conclusion causes broader memory disruptions across the entire video.
