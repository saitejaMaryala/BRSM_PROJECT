# Hypothesis 3 Analysis: Event-Middle Memory Effects

**Hypothesis:** *Recognition accuracy for Event-Middle (EM) frames is predicted to show no significant difference between the Natural Cut and Abrupt Cut groups, as these frames are not directly affected by event boundaries.*

---

## 1. Normality Assessment (Event-Middle Frames)
Before conducting comparative statistical tests, the participant-level mean accuracy for **Event-Middle (EM)** frames was assessed for normality using the **Shapiro-Wilk test**.

### Results of Normality Check:
As with boundary frames, EM frame accuracy significantly violated the assumption of normality.

*   **Participant Mean Accuracy (EM Frames):**
    *   **Abrupt Cut (AB):** Statistic = 0.9226, $p$ = 0.0001 (**NOT Normal**)
    *   **Natural Cut (NB):** Statistic = 0.9183, $p$ = 0.0000 (**NOT Normal**)

---

## 2. Statistical Comparison (Mann-Whitney U Test)
Because the data violated normality assumptions, the non-parametric **Mann-Whitney U Test** was utilized to evaluate differences in EM frame memory between the two groups.

### H3: Recognition Accuracy on EM Frames
*   **Natural Cut (NB):** $N = 87$, Median $= 90.00\%$
*   **Abrupt Cut (AB):** $N = 79$, Median $= 85.00\%$
*   **Test Statistic:** $U = 4087.50$
*   **Significance:** $p = 0.0322$ **(Significant)**
*   **Effect Size (Rank-Biserial $r$):** $-0.1894$

### Conclusion:
**Contrary to the hypothesis, a significant difference was detected.** The Natural Cut group retained Event-Middle (EM) frames significantly better than the Abrupt Cut group ($p = 0.0322$). This suggests that even frames not directly adjacent to boundaries may be affected by abrupt editing, possibly due to broader disruption of event structure.

---

## 3. Explanatory Notes on Methodology

### Why Was a Difference Found?
Although EM frames are not boundary-adjacent, the Mann-Whitney U test revealed a significant difference. This may reflect a more global effect of abrupt editing, where the disruption impacts memory for frames throughout the event, not just at boundaries. Visualizations (raincloud and bar plots) show that the Natural Cut group is more tightly clustered at high accuracy, while the Abrupt Cut group has a longer tail of lower scores.

### Interpretation of Effect Size
The Rank-Biserial Correlation ($r$) of `0.1894` indicates a **Small effect**. While the difference is statistically significant, it is less pronounced than for boundary frames, suggesting a subtler but reliable impact of editing style on memory for non-boundary content.
